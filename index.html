Why is it so hard to cancel subscriptions online?

Technically speaking, creating a button that shines enough to attract users attention can not be easier. We are so familiar with the buttons that are bold, attractive such as the“Buy” “Pay now” “One-click checkout” buttons.But, why is it so hard if you want to find a button to cancel a subscription? What is User Interface for? The following are some cancellation/unscribe steps examples from Amazon.com, The Economist, Grammarly.

Amazon.com : Almost everyone has experience of return and request a refund on Amazon.com. Have you anyone ever noticed the refund options? Why is the default refund method to refund to your Amazon account balance? Why is there not even a select option for “Refund to your credit card” even if the link is clickable?

The economist: Why do you have to contact them if you need to cancel a subscription?

Grammarly: why do you only have the renew automatically option? Why is the font so small when they mention auto renew? Why can not users have the option of only subscribing once and renewing later if they want?

In conclusion: As users, we enjoy the well designed web and the wonderful services online, and we love to pay for what we get. As service providers, can you offer easy steps to cancel a subscription anytime as easy as subscribing one? Users, who value providers' effort, time, need a one-click cancellation button as much as a one-click purchase/subscribe button.
